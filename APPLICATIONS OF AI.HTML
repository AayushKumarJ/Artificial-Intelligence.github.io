APPLICATIONS OF AI

COMMUTING:
 Google's Al-powered predictions, use location data from smartphones.Google Maps can analyse the speed of movement of traffic and suggest the fastest routes. Apps like Uber and OLA calculate the price of your ride, minimise the wait time, predict peak etc. by using Machine Learning. According to a pilot study's predictions, Al will shorten our commute even further via self-driving cars that will result in up to 90% fewer accidents, reduce the number of cars on the road up to 75%, and smart traffic lights that will reduce wait time by 40% and overall travel time by 26%.

EMAIL:
 Gmail successfully filters 99.9% of spam using machine learning. In the future, your inbox can reply to emails for you. Google has developed Allo, a new instant messaging app, which can use 'Smart Reply application to provide both text and emoji responses.

BANKING/PERSONAL FINANCE:
 Banks and other financial institutions use

Al to create Smart Apps to help customers deposit cheques without going to banks, identify financial frauds, etc. MIT researchers found that 'machine learning, could be used to reduce a bank's loss up to 25% in the future, a robot adviser will give customers sound investing advice and help them to make more accurate decisions.

SOCIAL NETWORKING:
When you upload photos to Facebook, it automatically highlights faces and suggests friends to tag by using ANNS. Facebook also uses DeepText, a text understanding engine. Pinterest uses Computer Vision, an application of Al where computers are taught to "see". Instagram uses machine learning to identify the meaning of emoji Snapchat uses lenses, which tracks facial movements. allowing users to add animated effects to their faces. In the future, social media apps will be able to identify fake news, messages sent wrong intentions, etc.

ONLINE SHOPPING:
Amazon uses ANNS to search for products and recommends products you may be interested in. Machine learning is also used for fraud prevention in online credit card transactions. In the future, online retailers will increase their revenue by helping you find and buy the products you are interested in. MOBILE USE A standard feature on smartphones today is voice to text. For example, you can click on the microphone icon next to the Search box and start speaking, and your phone converts the audio into text. Google uses ANNS to use voice commands for search. Microsoft has also developed a speech recognition system.
